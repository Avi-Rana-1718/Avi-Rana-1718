Every One Piece Manga Volume Has Sold Over 1 Million Copies Physically

According to Japanese tracking company Oricon, who tracks the physical releases of media in Japan, with the release of the historic 100th volume of the One Piece manga, all 100 volumes have physically sold 1 million copies according to the platform.

Another Oricon record shows that One Piece currently holds the title that it is the only series to have a volume debut at 1st place every year since tracking started 14 years ago, with Attack on Titan coming up second with a volume debuting at number one over 11 years.

In total, One Piece has printed (physically) and sold (digitally) at least 490 million copies around the world. Oricon only tracks the physical sales at some, but not all, retailers, meaning that it's likely each volume has sold more than one million volumes each.

My Hero Academia: World Heroes Mission Takes No.1 Spot for The Second Time

My Hero Academia THE MOVIE: World Heroes' Mission, the third anime film in the My Hero Academia franchise, rose from #5 to #1 in Japan in its fourth weekend. The film earned 204,210,550 yen (about US$1.86) from Friday to Sunday, and has earned a cumulative total of 2,407,196,800 yen (about US$21.9 million).

The film is the highest-earning among all three My Hero Academia franchise anime films, surpassing the 1.79 billion yen (about US$16.2 million by current conversion) of the second film, My Hero Academia: Heroes Rising. The film opened on August 6, and sold about 720,000 tickets to earn about 940 million yen (about US$8.51 million) in its opening weekend.
